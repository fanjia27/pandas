passing rate for Charter schools is approximately 95% while that for District is only 74%. We need further investigation to 
find out why Charter schools are better than District schools. There are some key factors we may consider, student number, 
school budget, student quality level.

2.When we take a look at the budget per student in each school, the district schools have obviously higher number than charter 
schools. In contrast, schools with higher spending per student ($626-$645) performed worse compared to the schools with smaller 
budget (<$585 per student). Actually when the school spending is the highest ($646-$675), the students’ test results are the 
worst. It demonstrates that simply increasing budget may not guarantee the improvement of students’ performance. 

3.According to the available data, the small and medium sized schools have more outstanding passing rates (94.8% and 95.2% 
respectively) than the large sized school (only 76.4%).
